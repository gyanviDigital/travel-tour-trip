Itinerary for 4 Nights 5 Days Nepal Tour Package

DAY 1

Arrival in Kathmandu/Tribhuvan International Airport

  • Upon your arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, you will be greeted by our warm and friendly representative. They will assist you with airport formalities and ensure a smooth arrival process.
  • After the airport procedures, you will be transferred to your designated hotel in Kathmandu. Check-in at the hotel and take some time to refresh and unwind.
  • In the afternoon, embark on a half-day sightseeing tour in Kathmandu, the vibrant capital city of Nepal. Your cultural exploration begins with a visit to Pashupatinath and Boudhanath, both UNESCO World Heritage sites, each boasting unique architectural and artistic significance .
  • Pashupatinath Temple: Pashupatinath Temple, located on the banks of the sacred Bagmati River in Kathmandu, Nepal, is one of the most revered and important religious sites for Hindus. This ancient temple is a UNESCO World Heritage site and holds immense significance in both religious and ultural contexts. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities in Hinduism, and is often referred to as the ‘Temple of Lord Pashupatinath. It stands as a symbol of Nepal’s deep-rooted spirituality and is a hub for religious practices, rituals, and festivals. Pashupatinath’s architecture reflects classic pagoda-style design with intricate wood and stone carvings. The temple’s golden roof and silver doors add to its opulence and beauty.
  • Pashupatinath is not only a place of worship but also a center for contemplation and reflection, attracting pilgrims, sadhus (Hindu holy men), and devotees from around the world.
  • The atmosphere is imbued with spirituality, and you can witness a wide range of religious ceremonies and rituals that take place daily, adding to the temple’s vibrant and mystical aura.
  • Bouddhanath Stupa: Bouddhanath Stupa, also known as Bouddha, is a prominent and sacred Buddhist monument in the heart of Kathmandu, Nepal. This colossal stupa is one of the largest in the world and serves as a profound symbol of Buddhist faith and heritage. With its striking white dome, radiant gold spire, and the watchful eyes of the Buddha, Boudhanath exudes a serene and spiritual ambiance, attracting both pilgrims and travelers. The stupa is encircled by a bustling enclave of monasteries, prayer flags, and shops, making it a vibrant and significant hub for Tibetan Buddhism in Nepal.
  • Pilgrims circumambulate the stupa in a clockwise direction, spinning prayer wheels and offering butter lamps, while visitors can soak in the atmosphere, visit the nearby monasteries, and enjoy the vibrant colors, rituals, and rhythms of this timeless sacred site. Boudhanath is not only a place of worship but also a center of cultural and spiritual exchange, where ancient traditions meet modern contemplation. After this captivating cultural journey, you will return to your hotel for an overnight stay.

DAY 2

Drive from Kathmandu to Pokhara via Manakamana

  • After enjoying a delightful breakfast at your hotel, it is time to set off on your After enjoying a delightful breakfast at your hotel, it is time to set off on your scenic drive to the enchanting city of Pokhara. Your journey is punctuated by an intriguing stop at Manakamana Temple, a sacred Hindu shrine that sits atop a picturesque hill.
  • Manakamana Temple: Nestled atop a lush green hill in the Gorkha district of Nepal, the Manakamana Temple stands as a revered symbol of spirituality and devotion. The temple, dedicated to the Hindu goddess Bhagwati, is renowned for granting the wishes of its pilgrims and providing a sense of profound tranquility. What sets Manakamana Temple apart is not just its spiritual significance, but also the journey one must undertake to reach it.
  • Accessible via a thrilling cable car ride that offers panoramic views of the Nepalese countryside and the Himalayan peaks, a visit to Manakamana is both a pilgrimage and an adventure. Whether you seek spiritual solace or simply wish to revel in the breathtaking landscapes, Manakamana Temple holds a unique allure that beckons travelers and devotees alike.
  • Continuing your journey towards Pokhara, you will witness Nepal’s picturesquecountryside, with terraced fields, traditional villages, and the winding Trishuli River.
  • Upon reaching Pokhara, you will check in at your hotel, where you can relax and rejuvenate after a long and eventful day. As the evening approaches, you have the opportunity to explore the serene lakeside city of Pokhara, with its delightful mix of cafes, restaurants, and shops. The stunning Phewa Lake is a serene spot to enjoy the beauty of the city. Your overnight stay will be in Pokhara, ensuring you’re well-rested for the adventures that lie ahead.

DAY 3

Pokhara Full-Day Sightseeing

  • Day begins with a beautiful sunrise from Sarangkot and visiting Bindabasini temple. Today, you will awaken in the mesmerizing city of Pokhara, often referred to as the “City of Lakes” and renowned for its breathtaking mountain vistas.
  • Sarangkot Sunrise: Begin your day before dawn to witness the enchanting sunrise at Sarangkot. Perched atop a hill, Sarangkot offers one of the most breathtaking sunrise views in Pokhara. As the first light of day paints the sky with vibrant hues, you’ll be treated to panoramic views of the Annapurna and Fishtail mountains. This magical moment is a photographer’s delight and a truly spiritual experience.
  • Bindabasini Temple: Located in the heart of Pokhara, this is a sacred and revered Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Bhagwati, a manifestation of the goddess Durga. This centuries-old temple stands as a significant religious and cultural landmark, attracting both devotees and tourists. Perched on a hill, the temple offers not only spiritual sanctity but also panoramic views of the charming Pokhara Valley and the Annapurna and Machhapuchhre mountain ranges. The tranquil and sacred atmosphere, coupled with breathtaking vistas, makes it a must-visit destination for those exploring Pokhara’s cultural and natural treasures. Next, you will venture into the mysterious depths of the Gupteshwor Cave, home to fascinating rock formations and a hidden waterfall. Nearby, you will visit Devi’s Fall, a stunning cascade with a unique history.
  • Devi’s Falls: Devi’s Falls, also known as ‘Patale Chhango’, is a captivating natural wonder located in Pokhara, Nepal. This waterfall is famous for its unique and turbulent path, which includes a sudden plunge into a narrow and mysterious chasm, creating a roaring spectacle of water.
  • Gupteshwor Cave/Devi’s Falls: This cave is home to remarkable limestoneformations, glistening stalactites, and awe-inspiring rock sculptures. Inside the cave, a narrow passage leads you to the stunning Gupteshwor Mahadev Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple holds spiritual significance and is a place of reverence for both locals and visitors alike. Devi’s Falls and Gupteshwor Cave make for a fascinating combination of natural beauty and spiritual exploration, adding depth to your Pokhara adventure and ensuring an unforgettable experience. Your journey will then lead you to the Tibetan Refugee Camp, where you can gain insights into Tibetan culture and history. One of the day’s highlights is a peaceful boat ride on the tranquil Phewa Lake, offering spectacular views of the Annapurna and Machhapuchhre (Fishtail) mountains.
  • Phewa Lake: This freshwater lake located in the enchanting city of Pokhara, Nepal. As the second-largest lake in the Pokhara Valley, it is a prominent natural gem that adds to the city’s charm. With the majestic Annapurna Range and Machhapuchhre (Fishtail Mountain) as its backdrop, Phewa Lake offers a stunning and tranquil setting for travelers and locals alike. The pristine waters of the lake reflect the surrounding mountains, creating a mesmerizing scene. Phewa Lake is not only a place of natural beauty but also a hub of various recreational activities, including boating, lakeside strolls, and a thriving lakeside district with restaurants and shops. This idyllic destination encapsulates the serene ambiance and striking landscapes that make Pokhara a must-visit city in Nepal.
  • Mount Machhapuchhre: Also known as Mt. Fishtail, this is one of the most iconic and sacred peaks in the Annapurna Himalayan range of Nepal. Its unique, fishtail-shaped double summit peak, rising dramatically into the sky, has made it a distinctive landmark and a symbol of the natural beauty that Nepal offers to the world. Machhapuchhre is renowned for its stunning symmetry, which remains unspoiled by human footsteps, as it is strictly off-limits for climbing to preserve its cultural and religious significance. This peak holds a special place in the hearts of both locals and trekkers, making it an alluring and mysterious destination in the Himalayas. The peak’s rich cultural history, along with its breathtaking natural beauty, continues to captivate those who venture near its vicinity.
  • The evening will find you strolling along the lakeside promenade, where you can immerse yourself in the city’s laid-back atmosphere and perhaps sample some local cuisine. You will spend the night at your hotel in Pokhara.

DAY 4

Drive from Pokhara to Kathmandu

  • After breakfast, it’s time to bid farewell to the enchanting city of Pokhara and begin your journey back to Kathmandu.
  • Check in at your hotel, allowing you some free time to relax and reflect on theadventures of your journey. The evening in Thamel is yours to explore as you wish. You can take the opportunity for relaxation, perhaps a good evening chit-chat, a relaxing massage or a book would help you rejoice and revive.

DAY 5

Kathmandu Half-Day Sight Seeing and Departure

  • Today, you’ll have a half day to explore the cultural and historical treasures of Kathmandu before your departure.
  • Budhanilkantha: Our day begins with a visit to Budhanilkantha, where you’ll witness the awe-inspiring sight of Lord Vishnu reclining on the coils of a cosmic serpent. This monumental statue is not just a feat of Nepali craftsmanship but also a profound embodiment of Hindu mythology. Your guide will narrate the enchanting tales and symbolism behind this magnificent deity, offering insights into the depths of Nepali spirituality. As you stroll around the sacred pool and soak in the ambiance, you will find yourself at the heart of a profound cultural experience.
  • Swayambhunath – The Monkey Temple: We continue our journey to the iconic Swayambhunath Stupa, affectionately known as the Monkey Temple. Perched high on a hill, this ancient religious complex provides a breathtaking view of the Kathmandu Valley. The site is inhabited by playful monkeys, adding to its unique charm. The stupa itself is a masterpiece of intricate artistry, adorned with the all-seeing eyes of the Buddha. Your guide will delve into the history, legends, and spiritual significance of this iconic structure. As you explore the premises, you’ll find yourself immersed in the traditions and practices of Nepali Buddhism.
  • Kathmandu Durbar Square: Our journey continues to the historic Kathmandu Durbar Square, a UNESCO World Heritage site that truly embodies a living museum. This square showcases an impressive array of historic temples, palaces, and courtyards, each boasting unique architectural and artistic significance. As you explore this historical treasure trove, your guide will transport you back in time with tales of the kings and dynasties that once ruled from this very square. The exquisite wood carvings, artistic masterpieces, and the vivid tapestry of local life against this backdrop will mesmerize your senses.

DAY 6

Departure From Kathmandu

  • After the sightseeing we will transfer you to the Airport as per your respective flight timings and with a warm farewell we move towards the furtherdestination with tons of memories to bring back to your home.

  • In Pokhara, explore Phewa Lake, Sarangkot for sunrise views, Davis Falls, World Peace Pagoda, and Bindhyabasini Temple.
  • Activities: Include outdoor activities such as hiking, boating on Phewa Lake, paragliding (especially in Pokhara), and exploring caves and waterfalls.
  • Cultural Experiences: Plan for cultural experiences like attending evening Aarti at Pashupatinath Temple, exploring the bustling streets of Kathmandu and Pokhara, and trying local cuisine at traditional Nepali restaurants.
  • Permits and Entrance Fees: Include any necessary permits and entrance fees for sightseeing attractions and national parks, such as Sagarmatha National Park entry fee for certain treks or permits for visiting specific cultural sites.

Exclusions

  • International Flights: Typically, international flights to and from Nepal are not included in travel packages and need to be arranged separately.
